Don not Smoke!

Smoking poses severe health risks, causing a myriad of harmful effects. It is a leading cause of preventable deaths worldwide, linked to lung cancer, heart disease, and respiratory disorders. Secondhand smoke harms non-smokers, especially children. Smoking also accelerates aging, damages skin, and diminishes overall quality of life, highlighting the urgent need for cessation.

Topic Information
Health Risks Smoking is a leading cause of lung cancer, heart disease, and respiratory disorders. It also increases the risk of stroke and various cancers.
Secondhand Smoke Exposure to secondhand smoke is harmful to non-smokers, especially children, increasing their risk of respiratory illnesses and sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS).
Addictive Nicotine Nicotine in cigarettes is highly addictive, making it challenging for smokers to quit. It leads to physical and psychological dependence.
Smoking and Pregnancy Smoking during pregnancy can harm the developing fetus, leading to low birth weight, premature birth, and developmental issues.
Long-Term Effects Smoking also accelerates aging, causes skin damage, and contributes to a range of chronic health problems, reducing overall quality of life.
